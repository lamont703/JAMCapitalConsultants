Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our pricing and services.

What's the difference between your pricing plans?
We offer three plan categories: Free Plan ($0/forever) gives you JAM Engine access, Dispute Library, and 2 lifetime dispute letter credits. DIY Plans ($29-$99/month) add monthly dispute letter credits (5-50 per month) with email/phone support. Done For You Plans (starting $197/month) provide full-service credit dispute where we handle everything for you, with 4 different payment options to fit your budget.
How do the DIY plan dispute letter credits work?
DIY Starter ($29/month) includes 5 dispute letter credits, DIY Professional ($59/month) includes 15 credits, and DIY Premium ($99/month) includes 50 credits. These credits renew monthly and let you generate professional dispute letters using our JAM Dispute Bot. All DIY plans include everything from the Free plan plus these monthly credits.
What are the Done For You payment options?
We offer 4 payment structures for the same premium service: Elite JumpStart ($399 setup + $197/month), Momentum Builder (2 weekly $250 payments + $197/month - Most Popular), Strategic Start (4 weekly $150 payments + $197/month), and Platinum Commitment ($1,499 one-time includes 6 months). All provide identical white-glove service with a dedicated specialist handling everything.
How long does credit dispute take?
Most clients see initial results within 30-45 days, with significant improvements typically occurring within 3-6 months. Complex cases may take longer. DIY plan users can work at their own pace, while Done For You clients get weekly progress updates and dedicated specialist management to optimize timing.
What's included in the JAM Engine Credit Management Platform?
JAM Engine is our comprehensive credit management platform included in all plans. It provides credit report analysis, tracks your progress, manages dispute documentation, offers personalized recommendations, and integrates with our JAM Dispute Bot for seamless credit dispute workflow. It's your central hub for all credit improvement activities.
Can I upgrade or downgrade my plan anytime?
Yes! You can upgrade from Free to DIY plans, between DIY tiers, or from DIY to Done For You service at any time. Downgrades are processed at your next billing cycle. The Free plan never expires and requires no payment information, so you can always return to it if needed.
What's the difference between DIY and Done For You service?
DIY plans give you all the tools and resources to dispute your credit yourself - dispute letter generation, credit analysis, educational resources, and support. Done For You service assigns you a dedicated credit dispute specialist who handles everything: writing disputes, sending letters, following up with creditors, negotiating on your behalf, and providing weekly progress updates.
Is there a guarantee for credit score improvement?
While we cannot legally guarantee specific point increases (no credit dispute company can), our methods have helped thousands of clients improve their credit scores. We offer a 30-day money-back guarantee on all paid plans. Done For You clients also get a 6-month minimum commitment guarantee - if you follow our program and don't see meaningful results, we'll work with you to make it right.
How does the JAM Dispute Bot work?
JAM Dispute Bot analyzes your credit reports, identifies potential errors or discrepancies, and generates professional dispute letters. Free plan users get 2 lifetime credits, DIY plans get monthly credits (5-50 depending on tier), and Done For You clients get unlimited usage as part of their specialist's service. It also includes credit education and strategic guidance.
What support do I get with each plan?
Free plan includes self-service resources and documentation. DIY Starter and Professional include email support, while DIY Premium adds phone support. Done For You plans include a dedicated credit dispute specialist, weekly progress updates, direct creditor negotiations, and priority support for any questions or concerns.
